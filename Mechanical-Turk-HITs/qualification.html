Instructions

We want you to play the role of a homeowner who is interested in programming interactions between devices in his or her home. Each of the devices is represented by a keyword, such as FrontDoor or HallLight. The interactions and state of the devices are also represented by keywords, such as OPENED or ON.

Task: Complete the logic for the given scenario using the supplied keywords. Several examples are provided below. Please look at the examples provided below for a better understanding of how the keywords relate to one another and how you should use the keywords to correctly express your answer.

Examples

Example 1: Simple Scenario

Scenario Key Words:

  • FrontDoor to represent the front door
  • HallLight to represent the hall light
  • CLOSED, OPENED, LOCKED and UNLOCKED to represent the states of the doors
  • ON and OFF to represent the states of the light
  • IF, AND, THEN, IS, and SET

Scenario: You would like to have your hall light turn on when you open your front door.

Correct Scenario Answer:

     IF FrontDoor IS OPENED AND HallLight IS OFF 

      THEN HallLight SET ON 


Example 2: More Complex Scenario

Scenario Key Words:

  • MotionSensor to represent your motion sensor
  • OutsideLight to represent your outside light
  • MOTION_DETECTED and NO_MOTION_DETECTED to represent the states of the motion detector
  • ON and OFF to represent the states of the light
  • IF, AND, THEN, IS, WAIT, SET and MINUTES to represent the various actions

Scenario: You would like to have your light turn on when motion is sensed, and then turn off after five minutes.

Correct Scenario Answer:

     IF MotionSensor IS MOTION_DETECTED AND OutsideLight IS OFF 

      THEN OutsideLight SET ON 

      WAIT 5 MINUTES

      THEN OutsideLight SET OFF


Example 3: Most Complex Scenario

Scenario Key Words:

  • InteriorGarageDoor to represent the door leading from garage into inside of house
  • GarageDoor to represent the car bay door on your garage
  • CarPresenceSensor to represent the presence sensor located in your car
  • CLOSED, OPENED, LOCKED and UNLOCKED to represent the states of the doors
  • PRESENCE_HOME, PRESENCE_NOT_HOME, PRESENCE_ARRIVING and PRESENCE_DEPARTING to represent the states of the presence sensor
  • IF, AND, THEN, IS, WAIT, SET, OR, UNTIL and MINUTES to represent the various actions

Scenario: You would like to have your garage door open when you arrive home and then close after either 5 minutes or when you enter the house.

Correct Scenario Answer:

     IF CarPresenceSensor IS PRESENCE_ARRIVING AND GarageDoor IS CLOSED

      THEN GarageDoor SET OPENED

      WAIT 5 MINUTES OR UNTIL InteriorGarageDoor IS OPENED

      THEN GarageDoor SET CLOSED
